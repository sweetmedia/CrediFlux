# Fonts — License Status

## Summary

The font files in this directory (`fonts/display/*.woff2` and
`fonts/mono/*.woff2`) are **TRIAL versions** distributed by **Dinamo**
(https://abcdinamo.com), a type foundry based in Switzerland.

**⚠️ These files are NOT licensed for commercial production use at the
time of this writing (2026-04-09).** They are being used in CrediFlux
during the v2.0 overhaul under a conscious, time-boxed legal risk taken
by the founder.

## Commitment to purchase

The CrediFlux team commits to **purchasing the full commercial license
from Dinamo before the end of Phase 1 (F1 cutover to production of the
new landing)**. The purchase will include at minimum:

- ABC Whyte full family (at least the four weights listed above)
- ABC Whyte Mono Inktrap Variable

The purchase is logged against the overhaul plan at
`/root/.claude/plans/prancy-hugging-dewdrop.md` as a precondition for
F1 cutover.

## What happens if we don't buy in time

Technical mitigation already in place:
1. Self-hosted fonts mean no external request to abcdinamo.com that could
   be monitored.
2. The font files are only served to authenticated users of the dashboard
   (behind login) — they don't leak to the public landing until F1.
3. If Dinamo flags the use, the fallback is to immediately swap to a
   different display family in `branding/tokens.css` (single-file change,
   propagates to both apps via `sync-tokens.sh`). Candidates:
   - **Inter** (free, open source, OFL) — decent humanist sans
   - **Satoshi** (free for commercial use with attribution) — geometric sans
   - **Space Grotesk** (OFL) — geometric sans with character

## Do not

- ❌ Do not commit the `.otf` or `.ttf` versions of these fonts to git —
  only the `.woff2` needed for web delivery.
- ❌ Do not redistribute these files to third parties (designers,
  contractors, etc.) — point them to Dinamo's website instead.
- ❌ Do not use these fonts in any client-facing project outside of
  CrediFlux without a separate license.
- ❌ Do not ship these fonts in any downloadable asset (PDF reports,
  email attachments, exported presentations) — PDFs generated by the
  backend must embed the font subset at minimum and carry this same
  license constraint.
